## AS/NZS 5033:2021 - PV ARRAYS (PUBLIC EXCERPTS)

### Installation Requirements Summary:
- **Minimum distances from roof edges:** Generally 1 meter
- **Cable management:** Cables must be protected from mechanical damage
- **DC isolator requirements:** Must be accessible but not in habitable areas
- **Array earthing:** All metallic components must be earthed
- **Fire safety clearances:** Specific setbacks required for emergency access

### Critical Safety Requirements:
1. **Roof edge clearances:** Minimum 1m from roof edges (varies by jurisdiction)
2. **Access pathways:** Clear access must be maintained for emergency services
3. **Cable protection:** DC cables require mechanical protection
4. **Labeling:** Clear labeling of all DC circuits and isolators
5. **Documentation:** Complete installation documentation required

## AS/NZS 5139:2019 - BATTERY SAFETY (PUBLIC EXCERPTS)

### Location Requirements:
- **Minimum distances:** Batteries must be installed away from habitable rooms
- **Ventilation:** Natural or mechanical ventilation required for gas venting
- **Temperature control:** Operating temperature limits must be maintained
- **Access:** Maintenance access must be provided
- **Fire separation:** Fire-rated separation may be required

### Safety Systems:
1. **Emergency shutdown:** Manual shutdown capability required
2. **Temperature monitoring:** Continuous temperature monitoring
3. **Ventilation systems:** Adequate ventilation for gas evacuation
4. **Fire suppression:** Fire suppression systems for large installations
5. **Warning signs:** Clear hazard identification and labeling

## AS/NZS 4777.1:2016 - GRID CONNECTION (PUBLIC EXCERPTS)

### Installation Requirements:
- **Isolation requirements:** Multiple levels of isolation required
- **Protection settings:** Specific voltage and frequency protection settings
- **Testing protocols:** Comprehensive testing and commissioning required
- **Documentation:** Detailed documentation and certification required

### Grid Protection Functions:
1. **Under/Over voltage protection:** Specific trip settings
2. **Under/Over frequency protection:** Frequency ride-through requirements
3. **Anti-islanding protection:** Prevents energizing dead networks
4. **Power quality:** Harmonic distortion limits
5. **Fault ride-through:** Ability to ride through grid disturbances

## COMMON INSTALLATION CHALLENGES (FROM FORUMS/REDDIT)

### AS/NZS 3000 Issues:
- **DC isolator placement:** Must be accessible but outside living areas
- **Switchboard space:** Adequate space for additional protection devices
- **Main switch accessibility:** Emergency isolation requirements
- **Cable sizing:** Appropriate sizing for DC circuits
- **Earthing systems:** Proper earthing of all metallic components

### AS/NZS 5033 Issues:
- **Roof edge clearances:** Confusion over exact distances required
- **Cable support:** Proper support intervals for exposed cables
- **Access pathways:** Maintaining clear pathways on complex roof designs
- **Penetration sealing:** Weather-tight roof penetrations
- **Structural adequacy:** Ensuring roof can support additional loads

### AS/NZS 5139 Issues:
- **Ventilation calculations:** Determining adequate ventilation rates
- **Room construction:** Fire-rated construction requirements
- **Distance calculations:** Measuring distances from habitable areas
- **Emergency procedures:** Establishing emergency response protocols
- **Monitoring systems:** Selecting appropriate monitoring equipment
